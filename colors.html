#Approximate Analogy

Pages 8-25

The way color is described in The Complete Color Harmony is very similar to how sound operates. In high school, my choir director always compared sound to color; which isn't a far comparison. The notes are hues (red or yellow to A or C), octaves are value (light and dark vs high and low), intensity/saturation is the volume (dynamics). But my choir director is not the first one to note this comparison, in fact, Radio Lab did a story on colors told through music a few years ago when describing how mantis shrimp experience color. Truly one of my favorite podcasts I've heard before, definitely worth a listen. Colors convey emotion similarly to how music can, though both are up to the audience's interpretation. Colors can allude to emotions of calmness, excitement, or anger, through greens, yellows, and reds (respectively); but music can allude to anticipation, sadness, and joy based on the key and tempo of a song. #Freeform

Pages 25-55, 176-191

My favorite color has always been red, thanks to Ariel in The Little Mermaid. I grew up wanting to be like Ariel in every way, I became a swimmer and I sang in the choir. I am not a fan of change, so my favorite color is still red. In high school I joked I just wanted to go to Nebraska or Wisconsin for the school colors rather than for the academic programs. Red is "compelling", "courageous", and "commanding" (Eiseman). But when asking my friends how they would describe me, they always answer "Yellow". My coworkers answered what color I embodied as an ice breaker during my onboarding, I became nicknamed Sunshine Girl because of how painfully obvious the yellow about me was. It is interesting that while I admire a color rooted in bold, daring history -- I know that I embody more of a sunny, hopeful, positive energy. When I think of my closest friends, the colors I think of have similar descriptions in their personality as they do in the color usage; for example my roommate is the definition of orange. Yes, she is a ginger, but she is also incredibly communicative, warm-hearted, spontaneous, and constantly in motion. Colors can describe more about us than we realize, whether in who we are or who we want to be.
