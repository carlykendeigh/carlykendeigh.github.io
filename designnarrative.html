Design Narrative

Inspiration for DOT:
I created DOT to be a solution for my personal life. I have struggled with staying on top of tasks and events my whole life, and have had to get creative in finding ways to ensure I get everything done on time. I have tried physical planners, Google Calendars, and writing on the back of my hand, but nothing was working. The best system I have created is Google Calendar plus a physical to-do list. After consulting some close friends I realized that so many people have a similar struggle. I knew I wanted to combine multiple productivity platforms by using camera scans and uploads to create digital events, an idea I got from my mother. The “dots” concept on every page came from my roommate, Ella, who really liked the idea of a digital bullet journal. I needed a solution for myself but then began catering the experience of DOT to other people. As someone who struggles in managing my tasks, I figured that I would be a good base user to test the designs on. I worked alone throughout this project. While any designer may be capable of creating a calendar/to-do list, not every designer would create it with the intention of being empathetic, fun, and keeping content from getting overwhelming. This app has been embraced by people in my life with neurodivergence, and while it benefits everyone, I especially want this app to be neurodivergent-friendly. As an ADHDer myself, I had plenty of experience and dopamine training to draw on in developing this application. DOT will improve the productivity levels of users once they are able to clearly visualize what tasks they have ahead of them, users will be more successful in their academic and career work, and feel more at peace because they will spend less time planning out their day and more time knocking out their tasks. Why Desktop?:
I am on my laptop most of the day, especially when doing productive tasks for school or work. Having my “home base” be in desktop format made sense for being the balancing point for working users. I would like to also create versions available on mobile, and watch to allow for on-the-go check-ins of daily tasks.

Story Inspired by UI:
My story that fueled this product was based around Josie, a college student who balances her class schedule with her on-campus job as a journalist for the Daily Nebraskan. She is optimistic and motivated but finds it incredibly difficult to keep track of everything that needs to be done. Having recently been elected as social chair for her sorority, Josie is becoming more overwhelmed and confused by her planners, calendars, and to-do lists every day. She is comfortable enough with technology since she uses it in her classes and profession, but likes to use it as a reference rather than her main focus as a paper planner. However, all of Josie’s school and work assignments come through on digital calendars, making her have to take the time to transfer them over. DOT is the solution for those like Josie. DOT takes a scan of her physical planner she takes so much care in making and recognizes the handwriting to convert each task, event, and color-coded highlight to a digital format where her calendars can be seen in a succinct format. When DOT doesn't just save time for Josie; it restores balance to her life. Josie sits down to do her work for the day, she can pull up DOT to see the tasks and events coming soonest and what she has to look forward to at the end of the week. The satisfaction of popping a bubble after completing a task and seeing the amount of work she has accomplished in her day is the most rewarding experience from DOT, giving users the time back they desperately need to take care of themselves. For Josie, DOT becomes more than just a productivity tool – it becomes a personal assistant tailored to her needs. With DOT, Josie not only gains efficiency in managing her academic and social commitments but also enjoys the luxury of allocating time for self-care – a factor often neglected in the life of a college student.

Creating the Design System:
You describe your process of creating the design system, highlighting a few choices (a few colors, your fonts, why you rounded only one corner on buttons, etc.). You do not need to justify every decision, just the ones you see are the most important and/or the ones you're proudest to contribute.
Colors My color scheme is very neutral and warm, it is meant to be comforting and calming, pleasant without being boring. The brown tones are meant to be calming similar to colors of wood and sand, which are earthy and familiar to all users. Most of the shades of brown on the paint actually variations of the same shade with different opacities over white, I did this because I had a hard time finding color combinations that were complementary with brown, a lot of the shades were too green for my liking, and it looked sickly.
Fonts The majority of the app uses the open sand font, which is a sans-style font. Very clear to read on digital formatting, but also with big enough letters that were spaced out far enough to be easily read as the text got smaller. The accent font is Abril Fatface, which, as the name so jokingly suggests, has very thick down, strokes in a slightly slightly fun house font. This is only used for the names of dates and months in the app. This is one of the only things the user is not in control of their schedule everything that they actually add to their app should be completely easy to read. Items that don’t change such as the month or the date or in the fancier font because they will be looked at slightly less.
Lowercase Test The entirety of this app is in lowercase, except for the events added in by each user. This is to reinforce the idea that the app is calming and easy to digest. The events that users input will be in whatever case they choose to write or type, but the content on the app is all lowercase.
Round Components In a similar fashion to the round, sans font, the buttons throughout the application have rounded corners. The rounded corners on components are relative to the size of the component, so they all have the same “percentage” of roundness. Even the icons for tasks and calendars have round corners or circles. The lack of sharp corners and edges also adds to the pleasant and relaxing aura of the app.
Dots Since the purpose of the app is “Done On Time” or DOT, having the dots on every page outside of the logo was very important to me. The lining of each hour block in the app is lined with dots instead of consecutive lines.
